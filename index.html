Queer Family Mediation

I’m Sarah and I’m registered with the Family Mediation Council as a mediator working towards accreditation. I specialise in working with LGBT people, although I am happy to work with couples and families outside this community as well. The majority of my work involves supporting separating couples in making arrangements for childcare and/or finances. In addition to this, I work with all types of family conflict, such as disputes between parents and adult children or between siblings.

Prior to training as a mediator, I practiced as a barrister for 13 years, including five years in financial services after gaining experience in family law.

Since 2021, I have worked as a counsellor for couples and individuals, having trained at the Institute of Family Therapy where I specialised in working with families and couples. My mediation practice is supported by my skills in both of these professions. From my counselling experience, I bring warmth, empathy and sensitivity; and from my legal background I bring sharp analytical skills and an ability to process complex factual information quickly.

With these skills, I am able to support people in reaching effective, lasting agreements. Mediation is considerably faster and less expensive than court proceedings and often leaves people with the sense that they have been listened to and have autonomy over decisions that affect them.
